The Story

Me the Beast

Built by a software engineer who got fed up with fitness apps that charged a fortune and still did not do what he actually needed.

Where It Started

I Built This Because Nothing Else Did the Job

Fitness apps are expensive. They say free but there is always a catch. $15 a month at minimum and half the features you do not even want. Being a software engineer myself, I could honestly build exactly what I needed and not rely on some developer that does not get it.

So I started this journey and built myself a custom GPT assistant and started building the features. After two months of testing i launched the custom GPT to the the public.

The custom GPT worked really well and so many people started using it. But I knew its limitations. So to take this to the next level I decided to build a real application, starting with the fridge and the cupboard. From there it creates meals for you. If you have meals previously created you can simply repeat them. If your pantry runs low it automatically adds to your shopping list.

People say they would die for their family. But will you live for them?

Built Around Real Life. Not Perfect Conditions.

Every single person is different. Some have kids, full-time jobs, and barely a spare hour in the day. Time is the biggest obstacle standing between most people and their goals. That's why during onboarding, Beast Coach walks you through a comprehensive set of questions to fine-tune and build a meal plan and workout program built entirely around your life not someone else's. If you don't have hours to spend in the gym, we're not going to pretend you do. The goal is simple: start. Because once you do, progress takes care of itself.

I'm not an influencer pushing someone else's product. I built this, used it from the very first prototype, and kept refining it along the way. Through my own journey I discovered something I didn't expect I became genuinely motivated. I wanted to compete in every challenge I could, pushing my fitness and building my confidence at the same time. That drive is baked into Beast Coach. You'll find a range of challenges to take on, a leaderboard to climb, and real people to compete against.

Pricing was something I wrestled with during the build. I knew I wanted all the AI features in there, but I also wanted to create something genuinely free not a watered-down version designed to push you into paying. The free version has no feature limitations whatsoever, except for AI. The AI version is identical in every way, but lets you quickly log meals, ask questions and log exercise through AI. There is a real cost every time AI generates a meal plan or answers a question, but I'm not here to profit off that. I'm not going to charge what other fitness apps charge. An annual subscription costs the same as a good meal costs at a restaurant.

Manifest is a powerful tool. Conquer your mind and the rest will follow.
