The Story

Me the Beast

Built by a software engineer who got fed up with fitness apps that charged a fortune and still did not do what he actually needed.

Where It Started

I Built This Because Nothing Else Did the Job

Fitness apps are expensive. They say free but there is always a catch. Fifteen dollars a month at minimum and half the features you do not even want. Being a software engineer myself, I could honestly build exactly what I needed and not rely on some developer that does not get it.

So I started this journey and built myself a custom AI assistant. I created a pantry. I scanned all the items in the pantry with the macros and simply asked AI to build me a meal. Even AI can get it wrong. Logging food varies depending on how you cook it, whether you weigh it raw or cooked. So many variables. None of these apps can actually do the job properly.

The custom AI worked really well and so many people started using it. But I knew its limitations. To take this to the next level I decided to build a proper application, starting with the fridge and the cupboard. From there it creates meals for you. If you have meals previously created you can simply repeat them. If your pantry runs low it automatically adds to your shopping list.

People say they would die for their family. But will you live for them?

Built Around Real Life. Not Perfect Conditions.

I realised every single person is different. Some people have kids and full-time jobs. The biggest problem we all have is we do not have time. During onboarding we go through a lot of questions to fine tune and build your meal plan and workout plan just for you. We are not going to schedule hours in the gym if you do not have the time. The aim of the game here is to start. Once you start, progress will happen.

I am not some influencer selling someone else's product. I am a person that has actually used this product from prototype stage through the build cycle, modifying it as I went along. During my own challenges I realised how motivated I became. I honestly wanted to compete in as many challenges as possible to build my fitness and my confidence. So in this application you will get various challenges, go on the leaderboard and compete against others.

As for cost, I debated this while building the application. I decided to build an application with all the AI functions included but while doing it I thought I would make a proper free version for everyone. I want as many people to feel confident, happy and build a life. The free version has no feature limitations except AI. The AI version is exactly the same but allows you to quickly log meals, ask questions and log exercise. AI does have some cost associated with it every time it generates a meal or answers a question. I am not going to charge what other fitness applications charge. An annual subscription is the cost of one meal in a restaurant.

Manifest is a powerful tool. Conquer your mind and the rest will follow.
